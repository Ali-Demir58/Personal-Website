GameTech Hackathon

We organized the GameTech Hackathon on April 19-21, a game entrepreneurship competition
where teams develop their own game ideas based on a given theme within 48 hours.
The goal of GameTech Hackathon is to support the entrepreneurial journey of the games created during the competition.
We aimed to achieve this by offering networking opportunities, various trainings, and awards.
For this event, which saw fierce competition among 10 teams, we chose the theme of metamorphosis,
inspired by the saying, "The only thing that does not change is change itself." Once the theme was announced, the competition officially began.

19 APRIL


Afterward, we heard from Melih Gürel, the OGEM coordinator and founder of FUTURINN,
who spoke about OGEM. Following his talk, Kadir Danisman, the founder of SPEKTRA GAMES,
delivered an inspirational speech.


Afterward, Bertilda Hüsna Meriç enriched our
hackathon with a dynamic training session on gamification and AI.

20 APRIL


On April 20, Ali Cevat Ünsal conducted a storytelling training,
highlighting crucial aspects such as the importance of a game's story
and how to craft an engaging narrative.


Afterward, the teams had the opportunity to meet with mentors for
a total of four hours. Can Oral from Cultic Games and Mert Buran from Game Circle
provided valuable guidance to our participants.

On April 20, we concluded the day with a
training session on presentation techniques, led by Recep İlkbahar, a board member of Planeth.

21 APRIL


Our 48-hour competition concluded with
each team presenting their projects. Our esteemed
judges—OGEM coordinator Melih Gürel, Alice Games founder Dilara Yeşil Aydin,
and Rechange founder Büşra Bektaş—then evaluated the presentations and announced
the winning teams.
